Deep Learning for Pulmonary CT Analysis


Abstract

Recent few months have witnessed one of the most devastating epidemic outbreaks throughout the worldâ€”COVID-19. Like many other lung diseases (e.g., chronic obstructive pulmonary diseases), this infectious pneumonia can also be detected by chest computed tomography (CT). As a routine screening tool, chest CT has been widely adopted to reveal radiographic patterns of diseases. It is often of clinical interest to quantitatively analyze CT scans by first performing segmentation on volume-of-interest (VOI) and then extracting related features. Our project, mainly supported by Yulei Qin, focuses on automated analysis for lobule, airway, vessel and nodules.

Datasets


Binary Airway Segmentation Dataset (Pwd: 9vy4)
This dataset contains the manual airway annotation of 60 public CT scans: 40 CT scans from LIDC-IDRI and 20 CT scans from the training set of the EXACT 09 Challenge. Please download the corresponding raw CT data from these two datasets by yourself.

Solid State Nodule Classification Dataset (Zhang et.al, MICCAI 2020)
We build a small yet definite CT dataset (171 patients) called SCH-LND focusing on solid lung nodules (90 benign/90 malignant cases). Under the supervision of SCH-LND dataset, many hidden drawbacks of unsure data (484 solid nodules selected from LIDC-IDRI dataset) served for malignancy prediction are objectively revealed.
